Taylor Swift Albums


Captured by Imagination, Expressed by Music





"Debut Album"

Taylor Swift

Swift wrote Taylor Swift from her personal life experiences as a teenager. The songs on Taylor Swift are from the perspectives of a girl in an American small town, within the extends from high school hallways to rural backroads.

"Country Pop Album"

Fearless

Fearless's prominent themes are love and life from a high school teenage girl's perspective. Many of Fearless's songs are about starry-eyed romance and use imagery associated with fairy tales, such as princes, princesses, white horses, and kissing in the rain.

"Open Letter Album"

Speak Now

Swift originally wanted to name the album Enchanted. But since this record showcased her maturation and growth as an artist, they change it. Swift sees the songs as “open letters” to those she loves, those who hurt her, and those who she hurt.

"True Breakup Album"

RED

The album's title refers to what Swift termed the tumultuous, "red" emotions that were evoked by unhealthy romantic relationships. Its songs discuss the complex and conflicting feelings resulting from fading romance.

"Grown Up Album"

1989

1989 captures the feeling of having grown into your adult self, when all that is left to do is announce it to the world. The album spoke to a generation of millennials living through uncertain times, looking for stability as young adults.

"Most Cathartic Album"

Reputation

Taylor created Reputation as an effort to revamp her state of mind. Swift called the whole album "a metaphor". She described the album's aesthetic as "evening-night","all cityscape, darkness, full swamp witch."

"Love Letter to Love Album"

Lover

The album takes inspiration from her recalibrated personal life and after having adjusted her personal life, freeing herself from her public image, inspired by her first-time discovery of "love that was very real".

"Character Storytelling Album"

Folklore

Folklore is exploring points of view that diverge from Swift's life, third-person narratives.It's "a collection of songs and stories that flowed like a stream of consciousness."

"Intimate Narrative Album"

Evermore

Swift described Evermore as an offshoot of "the folklorian woods"—an escapist, cottagecore-inspired direction. Evermore lyrically incorporates nature, landscapes and objects in the sky, such as crescent moon, sunrise, comets, amber skies, precipice, willow, clover and ivy.

"First Taylor's Version Album"

Fearless(Taylor's Version)

It was the diary of the adventures and explorations [of] a teenage girl who was learning tiny lessons with every new crack in the facade of the fairytale ending she'd been shown in the movies. There are tracks "From the Vault", which are songs that did not make the final cut of Fearless (2008).

"Second Taylor's Version Album"

RED(Taylor's Version)

Red resembled a heartbroken person. It was all over the place, a fractured mosaic of feelings that somehow all fit together in the end.



"Meet me at Midnight Album"

Midnights

"Midnights, the stories of 13 sleepless nights scattered throughout my life." Swift revealed that the album is a "collection of music written in the middle of the night, a journey through terrors and sweet dreams."
